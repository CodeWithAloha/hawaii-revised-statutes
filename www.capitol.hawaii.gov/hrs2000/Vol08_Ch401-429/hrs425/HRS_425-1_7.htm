§425-1.7 Correcting a filed document. (a) A domestic or foreign general partnership may correct a document filed by the director if the document:

(1) Contains incorrect information; or

(2) Was defectively executed, attested, sealed, verified, or acknowledged.

(b) A document is corrected:

(1) By preparing a statement of correction that:

(A) Describes the document including its file date or attaches a copy of it to the statement;

(B) Specifies the incorrect information and the reason it is incorrect or the manner in which the execution was defective;

(C) Corrects the incorrect information or defective execution; and

(D) Is executed and certified by a general partner; and

(2) By delivering the statement to the director for filing.

(c) A statement of correction is effective on the effective date of the document it corrects, except as to persons relying on the uncorrected document and adversely affected by the correction. As to those persons, a statement of correction is effective when filed. [L 1995, c 198, pt of §1; am L 1996, c 182, §9]
