PART III. CRIMINAL DAMAGE TO PROPERTY

§708-820 Criminal property damage in the first degree. (1) A person commits the offense of criminal property damage in the first degree if:

(a) The person intentionally damages property and thereby recklessly places another person in danger of death or bodily injury; or

(b) The person intentionally damages the property of another, without the other's consent, in an amount exceeding $20,000.

(2) Criminal property damage in the first degree is a class B felony. [L 1972, c 9, pt of §1; gen ch 1993; am L 1996, c 170, §1]
