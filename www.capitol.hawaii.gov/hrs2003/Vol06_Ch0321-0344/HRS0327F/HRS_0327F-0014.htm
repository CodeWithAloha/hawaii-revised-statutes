[§327F-14] Safeguard provision. Anyone who has good reason to believe that the provision of medical treatment in a particular case:

(1) Is contrary to the most recent expressed wishes of a declarant who was in remission and was competent at the time of expressing the wishes;

(2) Is being proposed pursuant to a declaration that has been falsified, forged, or coerced; or

(3) Is being considered without the benefit of a revocation which has been unlawfully concealed, destroyed, altered or cancelled;

may petition the family court for appointment of a guardian for the declarant. [L 1992, c 84, pt of §1]
