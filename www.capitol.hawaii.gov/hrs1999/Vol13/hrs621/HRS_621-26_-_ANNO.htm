Admission induced by promise of being "clear" held admissible. 10 H. 94.

Ruling conclusive if supported by more than a mere scintilla of evidence. 44 H. 352, 354 P.2d 986; 44 H. 403, 354 P.2d 960; 47 H. 158, 385 P.2d 830. Finding of admissibility not disturbed without showing of clear abuse of discretion. 51 H. 260, 457 P.2d 505.

Effect of voluntariness: Mental instability, amnesia. 44 H. 403, 354 P.2d 960.

Scope of preliminary examination on voluntariness where there are several co-defendants. 46 H. 183, 377 P.2d 728.

Statute applies only to confession of defendant on trial, not to confession of co-defendant. 53 H. 574, 499 P.2d 678.

No error in admitting statement where there is more than a scintilla of evidence supporting trial court's ruling that statement was voluntarily made. 58 H. 323, 568 P.2d 1200.

Effect on voluntariness: use of drugs. 59 H. 565, 583 P.2d 347.

Voluntariness hearing is also a constitutional right. 61 H. 198, 600 P.2d 1142.
