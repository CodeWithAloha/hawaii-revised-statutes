§560:5-421 Recording of guardian of the property's letters. Letters of guardianship are evidence of transfer of all assets of a protected person to the guardian of the property. An order terminating a guardianship is evidence of transfer of all assets of the estate from the guardian of the property to the protected person, or the person's successors. Subject to the requirements of general statutes governing the filing or recordation of documents of title to land or other property, letters of guardianship, and orders terminating guardianships, may be filed or recorded to give record notice of title as between the guardian of the property and the protected person. [L 1976, c 200, pt of §1; gen ch 1985]
