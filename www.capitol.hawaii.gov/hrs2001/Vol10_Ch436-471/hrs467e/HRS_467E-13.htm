§467E-13 Prohibited acts; penalties. (a) [2001 amendment repealed January 1, 2003.] No person shall:

(1) Use in connection with the person's name any designation tending to imply that the person is a licensed social worker unless the person is duly licensed and authorized under this chapter;

(2) Represent oneself as a licensed social worker during the time the person's license issued under this chapter is forfeited, terminated, suspended, or revoked; or

(3) Perform clinical diagnosis or psychotherapy unless the person is:

(A) A licensed social worker; and

(B) Registered as a clinical social worker as provided by paragraph (8) of the definition of "practice of social work" in section 467E-1.

(b) Any person who violates this section shall be subject to a fine of not more than $1,000 and each day's violation shall be deemed a separate offense. [L 1994, c 251, pt of §2; am L 2001, c 229, §3]
