§431:2-303 Examination of agents, solicitors, adjusters, promoters, and independent bill reviewers. [Section effective until June 30, 2002. For section effective July 1, 2002, see below.] For the purpose of ascertaining its condition, or compliance with this code, the commissioner may as often as the commissioner deems advisable examine the insurance accounts, records, documents, and transactions of:

(1) Any insurance general agent, subagent, solicitor, adjuster, or independent bill reviewer, including insurance agencies and surplus lines agencies; or

(2) Any person engaged in, proposing to be engaged in, or assisting in the promotion or formation of a domestic insurer, a stock corporation to finance a domestic mutual insurer or the production of its business, or a corporation to be attorney-in-fact for a domestic reciprocal insurer. [L 1987, c 347, pt of §2; am L 2000, c 288, §3]

 

§431:2-303 Examination of producers, adjusters, promoters, and independent bill reviewers. [Section effective July 1, 2002. For section effective until June 30, 2002, see above.] For the purpose of ascertaining its condition, or compliance with this code, the commissioner may as often as the commissioner deems advisable examine the insurance accounts, records, documents, and transactions of:

(1) Any insurance producer, adjuster, or independent bill reviewer, including insurance agencies and surplus lines agencies; or

(2) Any person engaged in, proposing to be engaged in, or assisting in the promotion or formation of a domestic insurer, a stock corporation to finance a domestic mutual insurer or the production of its business, or a corporation to be attorney-in-fact for a domestic reciprocal insurer. [L 1987, c 347, pt of §2; am L 2000, c 288, §3; am L 2001, c 216, §3]
