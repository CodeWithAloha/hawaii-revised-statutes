§607-7 Deposit and payment of fees and costs on appeal. [Section effective until June 30, 2006. For section effective July 1, 2006, see below.] All fees and costs required to be paid upon the filing of a notice of appeal from a court shall be paid to the clerk of the court from which the appeal is taken. The clerk of the court shall immediately transmit a record of the payments and deposits, along with a copy of the notice of appeal, to the clerk of the supreme court. The required fees and costs for an appeal, whether to be heard by the supreme court, intermediate appellate court, or both, shall be payable only once upon the initial filing of the appeal.

Where the appeal is from a governmental official or body other than a court, the required costs and fees for filing the appeal shall be made payable to the clerk of the court to which the appeal is taken and transmitted by the government official or body upon receipt to the clerk of the court to which the appeal is taken, along with a copy of the notice of appeal. In the event that an appeal from a government official or body is taken directly to the supreme court, to be heard by the supreme court, the intermediate appellate court, or both, the required costs and fees for the appeal shall be payable only once, upon the initial filing of the appeal. [RL 1935, pt of §3791; am L 1939, c 19, pt of §1; RL 1945, §9747; RL 1955, §219-7; HRS §607-7; am L 1970, c 188, §29; am L 1972, c 88, §5(1); am L 1979, c 111, §5(3); am L 2004, c 3, §3]

§607-7 Deposit and payment of costs on appeal. [Section effective July 1, 2006. For section effective until June 30, 2006, see above.] All costs required to be paid upon the filing of any appeal shall be deposited with the clerk of the court from which the appeal is taken, which deposit shall be transmitted to the clerk of the supreme court together with the record of the appeal; provided that the filing fee for an appeal shall be payable only once upon the initial filing of the appeal. The deposit shall be made at the time of filing the notice of appeal.

Where the appeal is from a governmental official or body other than a court, the required payment of costs for filing the appeal shall be made to the clerk of the court to which the appeal is taken except as otherwise provided. [RL 1935, pt of §3791; am L 1939, c 19, pt of §1; RL 1945, §9747; RL 1955, §219-7; HRS §607-7; am L 1970, c 188, §29; am L 1972, c 88, §5(1); am L 1979, c 111, §5(3); am L 2004, c 202, §65]

Note

Section effective July 1, 2006, does not include amendment made by L 2004, c 3, §3.

L 2004, c 202, §82 provides:

"SECTION 82. Appeals pending in the supreme court as of the effective date of this Act [July 1, 2006] may be transferred to the intermediate appellate court or retained at the supreme court as the chief justice, in the chief justice's sole discretion, directs."

Rules of Court

Dismissal for nonpayment, see HRCP rule 72(h).

Case Notes

Nondeposit of costs did not render ineffective a timely filed notice of appeal. 57 H. 168, 552 P.2d 355.
