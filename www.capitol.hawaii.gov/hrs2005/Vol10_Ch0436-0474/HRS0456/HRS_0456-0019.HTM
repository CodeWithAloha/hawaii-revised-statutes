[§456-19] Notary signing for disabled person. A notary may sign the name of a person physically unable to sign or to make a mark on a document presented for notarization; provided that the notary is satisfied that the person has voluntarily given consent for the notary to sign on the person's behalf, if the notary writes, in the presence of the person: "Signature affixed by notary pursuant to section 456-19, Hawaii Revised Statutes." beneath the signature, and if a doctor's written certificate is provided to the notary certifying that the person is unable to physically sign or make a mark because of the disability, and that the person is capable of communicating the person's intentions. [L 1995, c 141, §1]
